How to Read This Framework

The Three Layers of Understanding

Top (Why): The biology of hair loss. Hair follicles cycle through growth phases, and DHT shortens the growth phase while lengthening the resting phase, causing progressive miniaturization. This is the fundamental problem you're solving.

Middle (What's Broken): The bottleneck. Minoxidil requires an enzyme (sulfotransferase) to activate, and 40% of men lack sufficient enzyme activity in their follicles. Your ethnicity predicts your non-responder risk. This explains why minoxidil alone fails for so many.

Bottom (How to Fix): Three tiers of intervention. Tier 1 is the foundation (finasteride + tretinoin + minoxidil + microneedling). Tier 2 is what you add if Tier 1 plateaus. Tier 3 is cosmetic or surgical when medical therapy hits its ceiling.

Key Principles

  • Finasteride blocks DHT. Without it, minoxidil is pushing against unopposed androgenic pressure. This is why minoxidil alone fails when DHT is driving miniaturization.
  • Tretinoin converts non-responders. It upregulates the sulfotransferase enzyme that activates minoxidil. If you're a non-responder, tretinoin is critical. If your ethnicity has high non-responder risk, start it from the beginning.
  • Microneedling at 0.6-1.0mm. Deeper than 0.5mm. It upregulates enzymes, increases drug penetration, and stimulates growth factors. Every 4 weeks, not continuously.
  • The 12-month timeline is real. You won't see major changes in months 1-3. By month 6, shedding should stabilize. By month 12, you'll see if the protocol works. This isn't hype; this is biology.
  • All treatments must continue indefinitely. Stopping any medication causes regrowth reversal within 3-6 months. You're not curing hair loss; you're managing it.

Which Tier Are You In?

Tier 1 (Foundation) if: You're starting fresh, or on minoxidil alone with inadequate response. This is where most people should start. Cost: ~$130-250/month.

Tier 2 (Escalation) if: You've been on Tier 1 for 6 months and you're plateauing. Pick one or two additions based on your specific problem: oral minoxidil for crown-specific loss, dutasteride for stronger DHT suppression, PRP for growth factors, or LLLT for long-term support.

Tier 3 (Cosmetic/Surgery) if: Medical therapy has hit its ceiling (you've stabilized but aren't satisfied with density) OR you're Norwood 5-6 and medical therapy alone can't deliver the cosmetic result you want.

The Most Important Insight

Most men fail on minoxidil monotherapy not because they're "non-responders to everything" but because they lack either DHT suppression (finasteride) or enzyme activation (tretinoin). The full Tier 1 stack works in 94% of compliant users. If you're not responding, you're either not following the protocol perfectly, or you're in the rare 5-10% who need Tier 2 escalation.
